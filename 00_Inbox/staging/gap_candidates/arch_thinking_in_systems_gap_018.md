## Candidates bị bỏ sót

- [Concept] System Boundary: A conceptual limit imposed on a system for simplification, often artificial and mental.
- [Entity] Raw Materials: Physical resources (e.g., metals, fuels) extracted from the earth, forming part of a system's flow.
- [Entity] Stocks: Accumulations of resources (e.g., consumer goods, raw materials) that influence system behavior over time.
- [Entity] Landfills: Locations for waste disposal, contributing to environmental effects and potentially disrupting system flows.
- [Entity] Chemical Plants: Industrial facilities that transform raw materials into products, generating additional effects.
- [Entity] Ecological Footprint: The total impact of a population or activity on the environment, increasing with time.
- [Mental Model] Mental Boundary: A subjective demarcation used to simplify complex systems, potentially leading to unforeseen consequences.
- [Relationship] Cradle to Grave: A lifecycle assessment approach tracking a product's environmental impact from raw material extraction to disposal.
- [Relationship] Hydrological Cycle: The continuous movement of water on Earth, exemplified by clouds, demonstrating the interconnectedness of physical systems.
