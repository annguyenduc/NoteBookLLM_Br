# Lesson Draft: Introduction to IoT and Robotics with YoloBit

## Overview
This lesson introduces students to the fundamentals of Internet of Things (IoT) and Robotics using the YoloBit development board. Students will learn about hardware components, basic programming concepts, and how to control simple robotic functions through hands-on activities.

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Identify the main components of the YoloBit board.
2. Understand the concept of IoT and its applications.
3. Write and upload a simple program to control an LED on the YoloBit.
4. Explain the role of sensors and actuators in robotics.

## Materials Needed
- YoloBit development boards
- USB cables
- Computers with internet access
- Breadboards and jumper wires (optional)
- LEDs and resistors (optional)

## Lesson Duration
60 minutes

## Lesson Structure

### 1. Introduction (10 minutes)
- Define IoT and Robotics in simple terms.
- Show examples of IoT devices and robots in daily life.
- Introduce the YoloBit board and its significance in education.

### 2. Hardware Overview (15 minutes)
- Identify and explain the functions of key components on the YoloBit:
  - Microcontroller (ESP32-S3)
  - RGB LEDs
  - Buttons
  - Grove connectors
- Discuss the importance of each component in IoT and robotics applications.

### 3. Hands-On Activity: Blinking an LED (20 minutes)
- Guide students through connecting an external LED to the YoloBit.
- Provide step-by-step instructions for writing a simple program to blink the LED.
- Encourage experimentation with different blinking patterns.

### 4. Introduction to Sensors and Actuators (10 minutes)
- Explain the difference between sensors (input) and actuators (output).
- Demonstrate how sensors can be used to gather data and how actuators respond to commands.

### 5. Wrap-Up and Reflection (5 minutes)
- Review the key concepts covered in the lesson.
- Ask students to share their observations and experiences.
- Preview the next lesson on controlling motors and reading sensor data.

## Assessment
Students will be assessed based on their ability to successfully complete the LED blinking activity and their understanding of the basic concepts discussed.

## Homework
Research one real-world application of IoT or Robotics and prepare a short presentation for the next class.

# Scenario-Based Learning: Smart Home Automation

## Background
Imagine you are designing a smart home system. Your goal is to automate certain tasks to make life easier and more efficient.

## Scenario
Your smart home needs to automatically turn on the lights when it gets dark and someone enters the room.

## Tasks
1. Identify the sensors needed for this system.
2. Identify the actuators needed for this system.
3. Describe the logic flow for this automation (if-then statements).
4. How would you implement this using the YoloBit?

## Discussion Questions
- What are the benefits of this automation?
- What potential issues might arise, and how could they be addressed?
