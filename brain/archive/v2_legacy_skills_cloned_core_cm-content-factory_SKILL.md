# cm-content-factory — High-Conversion Content Pipeline (LITE)

> **Goal:** Create high-conversion content through behavioral psychology and the StoryBrand framework, turning strangers into advocates.

## When to Activate

- Writing blogs, landing pages, or email sequences
- Creating course content or teacher training narratives
- Optimizing existing content for conversion or engagement
- Building a content calendar from raw knowledge sources

## Instructions

### Content Pipeline Architecture

| Phase | Goal | Tool/Script |

### The Hook Matrix (12 Formulas)

- **Contrarian:** "[What everyone believes] is wrong..."
- **Curiosity:** "How does [X] achieve [Y] without needing [Z]?"
- **Data Shock:** "The shocking number: [X%]..."
- **Before/After:** "Before: [Pain]. After: [Dream]."
- **Villain:** "The enemy [X] is hurting you..."

### SB7 Narrative Framework (StoryBrand)

1. **Hero (Customer):** The customer is the main character — not the brand.
2. **Problem:** They face a problem (Internal / External / Philosophical).
3. **Guide (You):** You are the guide (Empathy + Credibility).
4. **Plan:** Offer 3 simple steps to solve the problem.
5. **CTA:** Direct or transitional call-to-action.
6. **Result:** Paint the picture of success vs. failure.

### Persuasion Weapons (Cialdini)

- **Reciprocity:** Free gifts / lead magnets.
- **Social Proof:** Testimonials, real numbers (e.g., "2,347+ teachers trust this").
- **Authority:** Certifications, research data.
- **Scarcity/FOMO:** Limited time/quantity (must be real — no fake timers).

## Quality Gate (Red Flags)

- ❌ Making the brand the "Hero" instead of the "Guide."
- ❌ Missing a strong hook in the first 3 seconds / opening line.
- ❌ Using fake scarcity (fake countdown timers) — destroys brand trust permanently.
- ❌ Missing a clear CTA at the end.
- ❌ Not auditing for SEO standards (Title, Meta, H1-H3 hierarchy).

## Example Triggers

- "Create a landing page for the Robotics course following the SB7 framework."
