# k12-shared-references — STEAM Shared Knowledge Base

> **Goal:** Provide the single Source of Truth for common educational frameworks used in STEAM. Other skills reference this document to ensure consistency in terminology and standards application.

## When to Activate

- Any skill needing to reference Bloom's Taxonomy action verbs
- Cross-referencing NGSS SEP/CCC codes or GDPT 2018 competency codes
- Looking up the correct 5E or EDP step definitions
- Verifying UNESCO teacher competency levels before designing a module

## Instructions

### 1. Bloom's Taxonomy (2001 Revision)

| Level | Description (K-12 context) | Suggested Action Verbs |
|-------|---------------------------|----------------------|
| **Remember** | Recall, recognize information. | State, list, name, identify. |
| **Understand** | Explain, summarize meaning. | Explain, compare, classify. |
| **Apply** | Use knowledge in exercises. | Practice, calculate, operate. |
| **Analyze** | Identify parts of a system. | Analyze errors, compare, contrast. |
| **Evaluate** | Judge, construct arguments. | Evaluate, critique, select. |
| **Create** | Produce something new. | Design, program, build, construct. |

### 2. Engineering Design Process (EDP)

1. **🚀 Ask:** Identify the problem & constraints.
2. **💡 Imagine:** Brainstorm solutions.
3. **🗺️ Plan:** Draw diagrams, select materials.
4. **🛠️ Create:** Build a prototype.
5. **🧪 Test:** Collect data & evaluate.
6. **♻️ Improve:** Optimize the prototype.

### 3. 5E Instructional Model

| Phase | Teacher Role | Student Activity |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Engage** | Create curiosity | Student makes prior knowledge connections. |
| **Explore** | Facilitate hands-on activity | Student experiences the phenomenon. |
| **Explain** | Introduce vocabulary/concepts | Student & teacher systematize knowledge. |
| **Elaborate** | Apply to new context | Student transfers knowledge to new situations. |
| **Evaluate** | Assess learning outcomes | Student demonstrates mastery. |

### 4. UNESCO Teacher AI Competency Framework (2024)

**5 Competency Aspects:**
1. Human-centered mindset: Control and accountability.
2. AI Ethics: Safe use, security, transparency.
3. AI Foundation & Application: Technical skills and tools.
4. AI Pedagogy: Integrating AI to improve teaching and learning.
5. AI for Professional Development: Lifelong learning and digital transformation.

### 5. Student Standards

#### 🇺🇸 NGSS 3D Learning

**SEP — Science & Engineering Practices:**
1. Asking questions and defining problems.
2. Developing and using models.
3. Planning and carrying out investigations.
4. Analyzing and interpreting data.
5. Using mathematics and computational thinking.
6. Constructing explanations and designing solutions.
7. Engaging in argument from evidence.
8. Obtaining, evaluating, and communicating information.

**CCC — Crosscutting Concepts:**
1. Patterns. 2. Cause and Effect. 3. Scale, Proportion, and Quantity. 4. Systems and System Models. 5. Energy and Matter. 6. Structure and Function. 7. Stability and Change.

#### 🇻🇳 GDPT 2018 (Vietnam)

**IT Competency (NLa–NLe):**
- **NLa:** Use and manage IT tools.
- **NLb:** Behave appropriately in digital environments.
- **NLc:** Solve problems using IT assistance.
- **NLd:** Apply IT in learning and self-study.
- **NLe:** Collaborate in digital environments.

**Natural Science Competency (KHTN):**
- Natural science understanding.
- Nature investigation (observation, experiment, inquiry).
- Applying knowledge and skills to practical problems.

### 6. Pedagogical Bridge Models

- **TPACK:** Technological Pedagogical Content Knowledge.
- **SAMR:** Substitution, Augmentation, Modification, Redefinition.

## Quality Gate (Red Flags)

- ❌ Never change competency codes (e.g., NLa, NLb, SEP 3).
- ❌ Every Science lesson must map to at least 1 SEP and 1 CCC.
