# k12-science-inquiry — Science Inquiry Activity Designer (PRO)

> **Goal:** Design Inquiry-Based Learning (IBL) science activities that help students develop "Natural Investigation" competencies. Transition from "listening to theory" to "doing science" through hands-on investigation.

## When to Activate

- Designing an IBL science experiment for a STEAM lesson
- Choosing the right inquiry level for a specific student grade/proficiency
- Writing a structured lab report template using the CER model
- Applying NGSS Science & Engineering Practices to a lesson activity

## Instructions

### 4 Inquiry Levels (Choose by Student Readiness)

| Level | Question | Procedure | Outcome | When to Use |
|-------|---------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| **1. Confirmation** | ✅ Teacher provides | ✅ Teacher provides | ✅ Already known | Beginning students, getting familiar with equipment. |
| **2. Structured** | ✅ Teacher provides | ✅ Teacher provides | ❓ Student discovers | Students developing core lab skills. |
| **3. Guided** | ✅ Teacher provides | ❓ Student designs | ❓ Student discovers | Students with a foundation — learning to design experiments. |
| **4. Open** | ❓ Student poses | ❓ Student designs | ❓ Student discovers | Capstone projects, self-directed research. |

### NGSS SEP Integration (8 Practices)

Each IBL activity must integrate at least 2 SEPs:
- **SEP 1 & 6:** Asking questions and constructing explanations.
- **SEP 3:** Planning and carrying out investigations (Core focus of IBL).
- **SEP 4 & 5:** Analyzing data and using mathematical/computational thinking.

### Lab Report Blueprint (CER Model)

Structure for student science reports — reference: `examples/pattern-lab-report.md`
- **Claim:** State the answer to the investigation question.
- **Evidence:** Cite specific data collected during the investigation.
- **Reasoning:** Explain why the evidence supports the claim using scientific principles.

## Quality Gate (Red Flags)

- ❌ **Cookbook Science:** Students follow instructions without needing to think, predict, or hypothesize.
- ❌ **Missing Hypothesis Step:** Students run experiments without first having a goal to test.
- ❌ **Qualitative Conclusions:** Using words like "more," "less," "faster" without actual measurement data.
- ⚠️ Always cross-reference with `k12-shared-references` for correct SEP codes and KHTN competencies under GDPT 2018.
